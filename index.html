Paying in the Digital Age: How E-wallets Have Redefined Convenience
Current Landscape of Digital Payment
    Electronic banking emerged in the 1980s in the country, marked by the debut of automated teller machines or ATMs. Initially, banks established three distinct ATM consortia, namely Expressnet, Megalink, and Bancnet. However, the efficiency of ATM services fell short of expectations due to the independent operation of the multiple consortia. This led banks to reconsider and launch inter-ATM network transactions. The outcome of this shift was the unifying operations across the entire banking system.

    Innovations in the financial sector positioned the Philippines as the pioneer in the introduction of mobile money services in 2001. Initially spearheaded by a telecommunications company, the groundbreaking concept aimed to extend financial services to the unbanked and underserved sectors. Through mobile money services, users could seamlessly perform fund transfers, remittances, bill payments, and purchases using their mobile phones. The initial mode of access was through the utilization of the SMS technology.

    Given the expanding young and tech-savvy demographic, these mobile money services held the promise of introducing these population segments to an uncomplicated and convenient method of digitally preserving value. These opportunities persist given that as of January 2020, around 63 million individuals in the Philippines were using the internet primarily individuals aged 16 and above. A significant portion of the population utilizes mobile phones with an estimated 75.66 million users in 2020. Concurrently, the Philippines topped the average internet usage index in Asia, with Filipinos dedicating an average of 10 hours daily to the internet.

    As mobile phones emerge as a practical tool for mobile banking and electronic payments, there has been a noteworthy increase in both the quantity and value of e-money transactions. This trend mirrors the continued transition towards digital payments.

    E-wallets have become increasingly popular in recent years. With the advancement of technology and the convenience they offer, it's no wonder more and more people are choosing to use e-wallets for their financial transactions. Not only do e-wallets provide a secure way to make payments, but they also allow users to store their payment information in one place, making it easy to make purchases online or in-store. Additionally, e-wallets often come with added benefits such as rewards programs or discounts for using the service. Overall, e-wallets offer a streamlined and efficient way to manage your finances. It has no doubt transformed the way people make transactions and manage their finances.

    In the Philippines, e-wallets like GCash and Maya (formerly known as PayMaya) continue to dominate the market. Filipinos benefit from the convenience e-wallets provide, however, it is important to note that while e-wallets have many advantages, there are also some drawbacks to consider.

    • One potential downside is the risk of security breaches or data theft. Hackers may target e-wallets to try to gain access to users' sensitive payment information.
    • Another concern is the reliance on technology and internet connectivity. If there are issues with the e-wallet platform or if you don't have access to the internet, it could disrupt your ability to make payments.
    • Lastly, not all merchants or businesses accept e-wallet payments, which can limit your options in certain situations. Despite these potential drawbacks, e-wallets continue to gain popularity and provide a convenient solution for many individuals.
Possible reasons why e-wallets can be considered as a form of disruptive technology:
Changing Payment Habits:
    E-wallets have shifted consumer behavior by providing a more convenient and efficient way to make payments. Users can store multiple payment methods in a digital format on their smartphones and make transactions with just a few taps.
    • Gen Z in particular, have increasingly developed an impulsive spending behavior, as TikTok to Shoppee ads can be enticing. Encouraged by the accessibility of easy payment through social media, temptation was common among users to spend on products. Their data is used to promote ads catered to their tastes based on how they interact with the media presented to them.
Financial Inclusion:
    E-wallets have played a role in increasing financial inclusion, especially in regions where traditional banking infrastructure is limited. People without access to traditional banking services can use e-wallets to store money, make payments, and even access basic financial services.
    • The COVID-19 pandemic forced the need for e-commerce while around a third of electronic transactions come from Metro Manila, most occurring outside that region. While Filipinos were initially distrustful of online payment and purchasing before the pandemic, 76% of Filipino online users committed to e-commerce transactions during lockdown.
Reducing Dependence on Cash:
    E-wallets contribute to the reduction of reliance on physical cash. This shift has implications for businesses, governments, and individuals, as digital transactions often offer benefits such as increased security, transparency, and traceability.
    • With the increasing preference for E-wallets, sensitive information regarding their money is subject to risk. While e-wallet services provide satisfactory security measures, most users are still victims of scams. Among Filipinos, the causes of these issues are careless installation of computer viruses and malware, weak passwords, and fraudulent schemes.
Integration with Other Services:
    E-wallets often integrate with various services beyond simple payments, such as loyalty programs, discounts, and peer-to-peer transfers. This integration enhances the user experience and provides additional value beyond basic financial transactions.
Mobile-First Approach:
    E-wallets are often designed with a mobile-first approach, leveraging the widespread use of smartphones. This aligns with the evolving preferences of consumers who seek seamless and mobile-friendly solutions for their daily tasks, including financial transactions.
    • To support sizable businesses, the Philippine eCommerce Association was established to assist them with security and further development in the market. Furthermore, support towards e-commerce has been spearheaded by the Filipino government with the Basta e-commerce, madali campaign, in support of smaller businesses and companies.
Disruption of Traditional Banking:
    E-wallets, along with other financial technology (fintech) innovations, have disrupted traditional banking models by offering alternative ways to manage money, make payments, and access financial services. This has prompted traditional banks to adapt and innovate to remain competitive.
